Van Gogh's Early Life

  • 1853 - Van Gogh was born on the 30th of March in Zundert, Netherlands. He was the eldest of six children, and spent most of his time out in the countryside, observing nature.
  • 1869 - He was apprenticed to The Hague Branch of the art dealers Goupil and Co., of which his uncle was a partner.
  • 1873 to April 1876 - He worked for Goupil in London from 1873 to May 1875, and from that point he worked in Paris until April 1876. Despite hating art dealing, being around artists and artworks on the daily aroused his passion for art, and he soon developed a fondness for Rembrandt, Corot, and Francois-Millet's artworks.
  • 1874 - He was rejected by a London woman. Due to this, he became depressed and solitary. He then worked as a language teacher in England.
  • 1877 - He then again changed jobs and worked for a bookseller in Dordrecht, Netherlands. He had lingering thoughts of entering the ministry as his father did, however, he changed his mind as he aimed to attend a short-term training as an evangelist in Brussels.
  • 1878 - After three months, he failed to get an appointment from the ministry. He then left to do missionary work in Borinage.
  • 1879 - He experienced the first great spiritual crisis of his life. He gave away all his belogings to the poor, and was then dismissed by the church as they determined that Van Gogh's acts were a too-literal interpretation of Christian values.
  • 1880 - Having given away all of his belongings and being dismissed by the church, Van Gogh fell into despair. By this point, he started to draw seriously, and realized his own passion as an artist. He then decided that his mission would be to bring consolation to humanity through his artworks.

  • 1881 - He moved to his father's parsonage in Netherlands and began to work from nature.

    The Evening Prayer after Millet, 1881

    He then settled at The Hague and worked with a painter named Anton Mauve. His exposure to other painters and artworks improved his knowledge of painting and by the summer of 1882, he was already experimenting with oil painting.
  • 1883 - He had the urge of being alone with nature and the peasants. This lead him to relocate to Drenthe, an isolated part of the northern Netherlands frequented by Mauve and other Dutch artists. He spent three months in Drenthe before returning home to Nuenen.

    Lumber Sale, 1883

  • 1884 and 1885 - During these years, his art grew bolder and more polished. He primarily painted still life, landscapes, and figures. During this time, his knowledge of painting quickly and immensely improved.

    Autumn Landscape with Four Trees, 1885

  • 1886 - He left to join his brother Theo in Paris. Van Gogh met with Toulouse-Lautrec and Gauguin, with opened his eyes to the latest developments in French painting. He was then introduced to other artists of the Impressionist group.
  • 1888 - Between the spring of 1886 and February 1888, his style began to change. His palette became colourful, his vision less traditional, and his tonalities lighter. By 1887 he was painting using broken brushwork that at times looked pointillistic. By the beginning of 1888, his post-impressionist style manifested.

    Starry Night Over the Rhone, 1888

    Throughout the year, he created pictures depicting blossoming fruit trees, views of the town and surroundings, self-portraits, portraits of Roulin the postman and other friends, interiors and exteriors of the house, sunflowers, and landscapes. Once hesitant to diverge from the traditional techniques of painting he worked so hard to master, he now gave free rein to his individuality.
  • 1889 - A physically and emotionally exhausted Van Gogh had an argument with Gauguin, and the events that transpirefd caused him to cut off his own ear. Though he was locked in th asylum, he was still able continue on with painting. During this time he created one of his most critically - acclaimed work: The Starry Night.

  • 1890 - He want back to Paris, longing to see his brother Theo. Eventually, he got into quarrels with Gachet and was overcome by feelings of guilt due to him financially depending on Theo. One night, he shot himself. Two days later, he died.

    Van Gogh never experienced the same acclamation that his works get nowadays. In his lifetime, he only ever sold one painting.

    Though he tragically died without ever knowing what his works could have amounted to, he undeniably inspired and paved the way for generations upon generations of artists around the world.

    For my part I know nothing with any certainty, but the sight of the stars makes me dream. - Vincent Van Gogh.
